Shiro Wat (Ethiopian Chickpea Stew)

Description

My family is Ethiopian Orthodox and in that tradition there are periods where you are supposed to abstain from eating animal products (essentially fasting). So, we ended up eating a lot of Shiro Wat. It is really easy to prepare, vegan and gluten-free, and hearty. Check it out!

  • Shiro Powder
  • Berbere Powder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Ground Cardamom
  • Cumin Powder
  • Olive Oil
  • Onions
  • Tomato Paste
  • Minced Garlic
  • Salt
  1. Heat a medium sized pot (or Dutch oven) on medium heat.
  2. Add olive oil
  3. Add a diced onion, and sauté over low to medium heat for a few minutes until fragrant.
  4. Then, add minced garlic along with tomato paste and sauté for a minute until garlic is fragrant.
  5. Add the shiro powder, salt, and three cups of water.
  6. Bring this to a boil on medium heat, then turn the heat down and let the mixture simmer for 20 or 30 minutes (depends on the strength of the stove and the consistency you prefer!) Make sure to stir at five minute increments, so it doesn't stick.
  7. Serve hot with rice or injera
